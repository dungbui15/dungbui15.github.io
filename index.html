Abstract

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Northwestern University, working with Xiao Wang. My research focuses on cryptography, including secure multiparty computation, zero-knowledge proofs, and post-quantum cryptography. I am particularly interested in bridging theory and practice: designing protocols that are both provably secure and efficient enough for real-world use.

I completed my PhD at IRIF, Université Paris Cité, under the supervision of Geoffroy Couteau. Before that, I obtained my Master's degree (2019–2021) in Cryptology and Information Security through the Cryptis program at Université de Limoges.

§1  Research experience

From summer 2025 to 2026, I was a postdoc at EPITA and the QI team, CNRS, LIP6, Sorbonne Université, working with Eleni Diamanti, Alex B. Grilo, and Ludovic Perret.

I spent the summer of 2023 as a research intern at COSIC (KU Leuven), hosted by Nigel Smart, and the summer of 2024 at NTT Social Informatics Laboratories (Tokyo), working with Masayuki Abe.

Before my PhD, I interned at XLIM in the summer of 2020 under the supervision of Duong Hieu Phan, and in the summer of 2021 at IRIF with Geoffroy Couteau.
